Methodology
The exact process — every step in order
The learning loop — one unit at a time
Run every step in order for each unit. The app generates tasks in this exact sequence automatically.
Step 1 · 3–5 min
Prime
Before consuming: scan headers/objectives only. Write one sentence: "After this unit I expect to understand ___." Activates context before you open the material.
Step 2 · varies
Consume
Work through the unit linearly. Do exercises. Flag "?" for confusion and "!" for surprises in a scratch note. Do not stop mid-unit — finish completely before Recall.
Step 3 · 10 min · do first if overdue
Recall
Immediately close the material. Dump everything you remember. Write: "The main thing this taught me is ___." List every "?". Only then reopen and check. Never skip this step — it is where learning sticks.
Step 4 · 10–15 min
Resolve gaps
Work through every "?" from recall. Try each yourself first. Use Claude for anything unresolved after 2–3 min. Mark "rabbit holes" separately so they don't block you.
Step 5 · 15–30 min
Apply
Build something using the concept. Course exercise? Do it, then extend it. No exercise? Minimal reproduction in your environment. Too abstract? Write exactly how it appears in your actual job — specific, not vague. Same day as consumption.
Step 6 · 10–15 min
Write unit note
Permanent synthesis note. Your own words only. Template: main idea (1 sentence), key concepts, gotchas, resolved gaps, work connection. Completing this step triggers your spaced recall schedule.
Unit sizing
The unit should justify the loop
Too small: you spend more time on the loop than the material. Too large: you can't hold it all in memory for Recall. For video courses: one thematic section (2–5 videos on one concept), not one video and not a full module. For books: one chapter. For docs: one major feature area.
Skill stages are automatic
Orient: set when you complete your first Prime. Study: shows unit completion fraction. Apply: set when you complete your first build session. Consolidate: set when you complete the synthesis task. You never click these manually.
Spaced recall timing
After completing Write note, recall checks are scheduled at day +3, +7, and +21 from that date — not from today. They appear at the top of Today on those dates, ahead of any new unit work.
Carry-forward warning
If you end the day after Consume but before Recall, the app flags a warning the next morning. Recall should be your first task before anything new. Memory decays fast after consumption — the longer you wait, the less the recall step is worth.
Doing more in a day
After completing Write note, the queue automatically regenerates with the next unit's Prime step. You can do as many unit cycles in a day as you want. Spaced recall timers always start from the completion date — doing multiple cycles in one day does not affect scheduling.
Book reading
Set any skill as your "book" in the Skills tab. Book chapter tasks appear automatically every 3rd completed unit cycle. Same loop applies: Prime, Consume, Recall, Write note. You define the chapters as units in the Units tab.
